# Brand Positioning Audit — Reference Examples

## Full Audit Example

**Brand:** Notion (illustrative example)
**Input:** URL only, no ICP provided

---

### Scorecard

| Dimension | Score | Evidence |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| ICP Clarity | 4/10 | "for teams" — too broad. Which teams? Doing what? |
| Value Clarity | 5/10 | "The connected workspace" — category, not outcome |
| Differentiation | 3/10 | Any tool could claim "connected" and "workspace" |
| Proof Credibility | 7/10 | "30M+ users" specific, but anonymous |
| Message-Market Fit | 5/10 | "Connected workspace" is how Notion talks, not how users talk |
| Competitive Separation | 3/10 | Asana, Monday, Coda could all say "connected workspace" |
| **Overall** | **4.5/10** | |

### Root Diagnosis
**Root failure: Differentiation**

The claim "connected workspace" describes a category of tools, not a specific reason to choose Notion. Asana, Coda, Monday, ClickUp, and Confluence could all make this claim. Until the differentiation is owned, every other dimension is constrained — you can't have clear ICP or message-market fit when you don't know what you stand for.

### Evidence (verbatim)
- Hero: "The connected workspace"
- Sub: "One tool for your whole team. Write, plan, share."
- CTA: "Get Notion free"

What's missing: specific outcome, specific ICP, any reason why Notion vs alternatives.

### Priority Fixes
1. **Own "blocks as atoms"** — no other tool has this architecture. Notion's real differentiation is flexibility through composability. The current messaging buries it.
2. **Narrow ICP by use case** — "For teams that build their own tools without needing engineering" is more ownable than "for teams."
3. **Lead with outcome** — "Replace your wiki, project manager, and docs with one tool" is clearer than "connected workspace."

### Rewrite Pack

**Option A — Benefit-forward:**
- Headline: "Replace five tools with one. Without losing any of them."
- Sub: "Notion adapts to how your team actually works. Build your own workspace in minutes."
- CTA: "Build yours free"

**Option B — ICP-specific:**
- Headline: "For teams that outgrow their tools every year."
- Sub: "One workspace that grows with you — from 2 people to 2,000."
- CTA: "Start for free"

**Option C — Differentiation-led:**
- Headline: "Every other workspace locks you in. Notion builds to your process."
- Sub: "Blocks, databases, docs. Mix them however your team works."
- CTA: "See how it works"

## Before/After: Root Failure Types

### Clarity Failure (Score: 2/10 before)

**Before:** "Empowering businesses with intelligent solutions."

Problems: No ICP, no value, no differentiation. Applies to every company on earth.

**After:** "Automate your sales follow-ups so no deal goes cold. For solo consultants closing $25K+ contracts."

Fixed: Specific ICP (solo consultants), specific outcome (deals don't go cold), specific context (high-value contracts).

---

### Language Failure (Score: 5/10 — good insight, bad words)

**Before:** "Our holistic platform facilitates organizational alignment through AI-powered workflow optimization."

Problems: Correct insight (AI helps alignment) expressed in inside-out language.

**After:** "When your team doesn't know what everyone else is working on, priorities collide. We fix that with 5 minutes of setup."

Fixed: Same insight, outside-in language. Uses how users describe the problem, not how the company describes the solution.

---

### Proof Failure (Score: 4/10 before)

**Before:** "Trusted by hundreds of satisfied customers."

Proof score: 2/10. Anonymous count, "satisfied" is vague assertion.

**After:** "217 marketing teams at mid-market SaaS companies run their entire content calendar here. Typical result: 40% fewer missed deadlines."

Proof score: 9/10. Specific count, specific ICP, specific outcome with a number.

## Competitor Benchmark Example

**Brand vs. Competitor Side-by-Side:**

| Dimension | Acme (brand) | Rival (competitor) | Advantage |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|-----------|
| ICP Clarity | 6 | 4 | **Acme** — more specific ICP |
| Value Clarity | 5 | 7 | Rival — clearer outcome claim |
| Differentiation | 4 | 4 | Tie — both generic |
| Proof Credibility | 8 | 5 | **Acme** — specific case studies |
| Message-Market Fit | 5 | 6 | Rival — slightly better language |
| Competitive Separation | 3 | 3 | Tie — neither stakes a claim |

**Finding:** Acme's clearest advantage is Proof Credibility. The rewrite should exploit this — lead with the case study outcome, not the category claim.

**Recommended headline direction:** "How [named client] cut support tickets by 47% in 30 days with [Acme]."

## Common Score Errors to Avoid

### Score inflation
Bad: Giving 6/10 for "enterprise-ready AI platform" because "it at least says AI."
Correct: 2/10 — any competitor says "enterprise-ready." No ICP, no outcome, no differentiation.

### Scoring what should be vs. what is
Bad: "Their value proposition could be about automation..." (inferring)
Correct: Only score what's actually on the page. Absence of evidence = evidence of absence.

### Root failure list vs. single root
Bad: "Root failures: ICP, differentiation, language, proof."
Correct: "Root failure: Differentiation. Fix this first — ICP and language failures are downstream of not knowing what position to own."

### Generic strategic risks
Bad: "If this isn't fixed, competitors may take advantage."
Correct: "If differentiation isn't addressed in 6 months, [specific competitor with same category claim] will own the 'connected workspace' position by default. They're already spending $2M/month in search ads against that term."
